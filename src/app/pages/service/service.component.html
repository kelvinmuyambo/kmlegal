LEGAL CHARGES

As professionals, we are entitled to remuneration in the form of fees. Legal services are unfortunately not cheap, a fact that all our clients are advised of from inception. Our legal charges are in accordance with and guided by the Law Society of Zimbabwe minimum fees tariffs. Charges are determined by the circumstances of the case and in particular the following factors:

  1. The complexity of the matter or difficulty or novelty of the questions raised;
  2. The skill, labour, specialized knowledge and responsibility involved on the part of the Legal Practitioner;
  3. The number and importance of the documents prepared or perused with regard to length;
  4. The place where and the circumstances in which the business or any part thereof is transacted;
  5. The time expended by the Legal Practitioner;
  6. Where money or property is involved, its amount or value;
  7. Any tariff of recommended charges issued by the Council;

The Law Society fees Tariffs are available for perusal by clients upon request.


DEPOSITS

It is our practice to request from time to time payment of initial and interim deposits in advance to cover foreseeable fees and disbursements. However the amount of the deposit requested is not an estimate of the total legal costs involved in concluding the mandate.

INTERIM ACCOUNT

If at any time our clients are concerned as to the amount of fees they have incurred, they are at liberty to ask for an interim statement. In any event, we send out interim accounts from time to time where appropriate.
